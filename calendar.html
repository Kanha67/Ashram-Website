Annual Sacred Festivals

Maha Shivaratri February/March

The great night of Lord Shiva, celebrated on the 13th night/14th day of the waning moon in the Hindu month of Phalguna. This is the most auspicious festival at our ashram, with 24-hour continuous chanting, special abhishekam ceremonies, and deep meditation practices.

108 Rudrabhishekam
Night-long Meditation
Sacred Havan

Navaratri September/October

Nine nights dedicated to the Divine Mother, celebrated in autumn. Each day honors a different aspect of the goddess. Our ashram conducts special pujas, bhajans, and spiritual discourses during this powerful time of shakti awakening.

Goddess Worship
Sacred Bhajans
Garba Dance

Guru Purnima July

Full moon day dedicated to honoring spiritual teachers and the guru tradition. This day marks the beginning of the monsoon season when sages traditionally stayed in one place to teach disciples. We celebrate with guru puja, pada puja (washing of guru's feet), and special teachings.

Guru Puja
Wisdom Teachings
Pada Puja

Krishna Janmashtami August/September

Celebration of Lord Krishna's birth, observed on the eighth day (Ashtami) of the dark fortnight in the month of Shravan. We celebrate with devotional singing, readings from the Bhagavad Gita, and meditation on Krishna's teachings of selfless action.

Birth Celebration
Krishna Bhajans
Gita Discourse

Diwali October/November

Festival of lights symbolizing the victory of light over darkness. We celebrate with a special Lakshmi puja for abundance, lighting of lamps throughout the ashram, and meditation on the inner light that dispels ignorance.

Lamp Lighting
Lakshmi Puja
Community Aarti
