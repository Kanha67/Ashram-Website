About Our Ashram

Our Sacred Journey

Shiva Shakti Ashram is a spiritual sanctuary nestled in the serene landscapes near the holy Pashupatinath Temple in Kathmandu, Nepal. Founded in 1985 by revered spiritual master Swami Shivananda Giri, our ashram has been a beacon of divine light and spiritual wisdom for nearly four decades.

The ashram is dedicated to Lord Shiva, the supreme deity of transformation and the destroyer of ignorance. Here, devotees experience the divine presence through meditation, yoga, and sacred rituals (pujas) that have been preserved and practiced according to ancient Vedic traditions.

Our community consists of dedicated saints, yogis, and spiritual seekers from across the globe who come to immerse themselves in the wisdom of the ancient scriptures and the transformative power of spiritual practices.

Our Founder

Swami Shivananda Giri

Spiritual Master & Founder

"The path to divine consciousness begins with understanding that Lord Shiva resides within each one of us. Our spiritual journey is simply the process of unveiling this truth and living in harmony with our divine nature."

Swami Shivananda Giri was born in the foothills of the Himalayas and started his spiritual journey at the tender age of seven. After years of rigorous spiritual practices in solitude and studying under renowned masters, he established Shiva Shakti Ashram to share the transformative wisdom of the Vedas and Tantras with sincere seekers.

His teachings emphasize the unity of all religious paths and the importance of direct spiritual experience through meditation and devotion. Under his guidance, the ashram has grown into a vibrant spiritual community that serves thousands of devotees annually.

Our Spiritual Journey

1985

Founding of the Ashram

Swami Shivananda Giri established Shiva Shakti Ashram with a small temple and meditation hall.

1990

First Shiva Temple Consecration

The main temple dedicated to Lord Shiva was built and consecrated with a 108-hour continuous chanting ceremony.

1998

Expansion of Facilities

The ashram expanded to include residential quarters, a larger meditation hall, and a Vedic library.

2005

International Recognition

The ashram began hosting international retreats and gained recognition for authentic spiritual teachings.

2012

Establishment of Yoga School

A traditional yoga school was established offering authentic teachings of Hatha, Raja, and Kundalini Yoga.

2018

Community Outreach Programs

The ashram expanded its mission to include humanitarian efforts, providing free education and meals to underprivileged children.

Present

Continuing the Legacy

Today, the ashram continues to be a beacon of spiritual wisdom, serving seekers from all walks of life and all corners of the world.

Our Spiritual Practices

Meditation

Daily guided meditation sessions in our serene meditation hall, focusing on various techniques including mantra meditation and Shiva dhyana.

Pujas & Rituals

Traditional Vedic ceremonies and pujas performed by experienced priests, including the sacred Rudra Abhishekam to Lord Shiva.

Scriptural Study

Regular classes on the Vedas, Upanishads, Bhagavad Gita, and Shiva Sutras to deepen understanding of spiritual wisdom.

Yoga

Daily yoga sessions including asanas, pranayama, and yoga nidra, designed to purify the body and prepare it for deeper spiritual practices.

Seva (Selfless Service)

Opportunities to engage in karma yoga through service to the ashram and local community, cultivating humility and compassion.

Bhajans & Kirtans

Devotional singing and chanting sessions that open the heart and create a profound connection with the divine.
