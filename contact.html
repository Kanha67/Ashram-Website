Frequently Asked Questions

Can I visit the ashram without an appointment?

Yes, visitors are welcome during our regular visiting hours. However, if you wish to meet with specific teachers or attend private ceremonies, we recommend scheduling an appointment in advance.

How can I stay at the ashram for a spiritual retreat?

We offer residential stays ranging from 3 days to 3 months. For residential stays, please email us at programs@shivashaktiashram.org with your preferred dates, duration, and purpose of stay. We recommend booking at least 4 weeks in advance.

What languages are spoken at the ashram?

Our main languages are Nepali, Hindi, and English. Most of our teachers and staff speak English fluently, and our programs are conducted in both English and Nepali.

How can I support the ashram if I cannot visit in person?

There are many ways to support our work from a distance. You can make a donation, sponsor a program, or participate in our virtual meditation sessions. Visit our Donate page for more information on how to contribute.

Is the ashram accessible to people with disabilities?

We have made efforts to make our main areas accessible, including ramps and ground-floor accommodation options. Please inform us of any specific requirements when planning your visit, and we will do our best to accommodate your needs.
